## üîÑ HOW THE SYSTEM WORKS

### The Three-Layer Architecture
1. **Slash Commands** (`/command-name`) - Entry points that inject context and direction
2. **Context Files** - Domain knowledge, patterns, and guidelines
3. **Agents** - Execute tasks with loaded context

### Workflow Execution
```
User Input ‚Üí Slash Command ‚Üí Context Injection ‚Üí Agent Execution ‚Üí Output
```

**Example Flow:**
- User: `/blog-post "AI productivity tips"`
- System loads: `essential-patterns.md` + `brand-voice.md` + `blog-patterns.md`
- Agent receives: Combined context + user request + execution instructions
- Agent produces: Structured blog post following loaded patterns

### Key Principle: Context Before Execution
- Slash commands are **context loaders**, not just shortcuts
- They inject specific knowledge, voice, and patterns into the agent
- Agents receive rich context, not just a simple prompt

## üìÇ CONTEXT FILES (`.opencode/context/`)

### Purpose: Inject Domain Knowledge Into Agents
Context files are the "brain" of your system - they contain patterns, rules, examples, and guidelines that shape how agents behave.

### Required Context Files Structure

1. **core/essential-patterns.md** - Universal quality standards and methodologies
2. **content/brand-voice.md** - Tone, personality, and writing style guidelines  
3. **content/blog-patterns.md** - Blog post structure, SEO, and formatting rules

### Context File Best Practices
- **Length:** 50-150 lines per file (focused but comprehensive)
- **Format:** Mix of rules, examples, and templates
- **Content:** Specific patterns, not general advice
- **Updates:** Version control changes to track what works

### How Context Gets Injected
```
Slash Command ‚Üí @file-references ‚Üí Content Loaded ‚Üí Agent Receives Context

/blog-post "AI tips"
```

# Brand Voice - Tone & Personality Guidelines

## Voice Identity

### Core Personality
**Professional Pragmatist** - Smart, helpful, and refreshingly direct

### Voice Characteristics
- **Knowledgeable but not condescending** - Share expertise without talking down
- **Practical over theoretical** - Focus on what actually works
- **Curious and questioning** - Ask "why" and "how" to dig deeper
- **Optimistically realistic** - Acknowledge challenges while focusing on solutions

## Tone Guidelines

### Primary Tone (80% of content)
- Conversational yet credible
- Helpful and supportive
- Direct without being blunt
- Enthusiastic about solutions

**Example:** "Here's what I've learned after testing 12 different productivity apps..."

### Secondary Tone (20% of content)
- Slightly more formal for technical topics
- Authoritative when citing research
- Empathetic when discussing challenges

**Example:** "The research on attention spans is clear, but here's why it matters for your daily work..."

## Language Patterns

### Words We Use
- "Here's how..." (action-oriented)
- "What I've found..." (personal experience)
- "The key insight..." (highlighting value)
- "This changes when..." (nuanced thinking)
- "Worth noting..." (additional context)

### Words We Avoid
- "Obviously" or "clearly" (assumes knowledge)
- "Simply" or "just" (minimizes complexity)
- "Everyone knows" (excludes readers)
- "Game-changer" (overused hyperbole)

## Writing Style

# Goals & Routines - Personal Priorities & Life Organization

## Core Life Priorities

### Primary Focus Areas
1. **Professional Growth** - Building systems that scale and sharing knowledge
2. **Health & Wellness** - Maintaining energy and mental clarity for long-term performance  
3. **Relationships** - Investing time in meaningful connections
4. **Learning & Development** - Staying current with technology and business trends
5. **Financial Security** - Building sustainable income streams and assets

### Monthly Goal Framework
- **1 Major Project** - Primary focus requiring 60%+ of discretionary time
- **2-3 Supporting Goals** - Complementary activities that reinforce main project
- **1 Personal Goal** - Health, relationship, or skill-building focus
- **1 Learning Goal** - New skill, tool, or knowledge area

## Daily Routines

### Morning Routine (6:00-8:00 AM)
- **6:00-6:30**: Wake up, hydration, light stretching
- **6:30-7:00**: Coffee + review daily priorities and calendar
- **7:00-7:30**: Most important task (MIT) - deep work when energy is highest
- **7:30-8:00**: Breakfast + news/industry updates

### Work Block Structure  
- **Deep Work Blocks**: 90-120 minutes with 15-minute breaks
- **Communication Windows**: 30-45 minutes for email/messages (10am, 2pm, 5pm)
- **Planning Time**: 15 minutes at start/end of day
- **Buffer Time**: 30 minutes between major commitments

### Evening Routine (8:00-10:00 PM)
- **8:00-8:30**: Dinner + family time (no devices)
- **8:30-9:30**: Personal projects or relaxation
- **9:30-10:00**: Tomorrow's planning + reflection
- **10:00**: Wind-down routine, no screens

### Weekly Rhythms
- **Monday**: Week planning + priority setting
- **Tuesday-Thursday**: Deep work on primary goals  
- **Friday**: Week review + content creation
- **Saturday**: Personal projects + relationship time
- **Sunday**: Rest + preparation for upcoming week

## Energy Management

### Peak Performance Windows
- **High Energy (8-11 AM)**: Creative work, writing, complex problem-solving
- **Medium Energy (11 AM-2 PM)**: Meetings, communication, routine tasks
- **Low Energy (2-4 PM)**: Administrative work, planning, learning
- **Recovery Energy (4-6 PM)**: Strategic thinking, content consumption

### Context Switching Minimization
- **Batch similar tasks** - All writing in one block, all meetings in another
- **Theme days** - Monday for planning, Tuesday-Wednesday for creation, etc.
- **Communication boundaries** - Designated times for email, messages, calls
- **Tool consolidation** - Minimize app switching within work sessions

## Decision-Making Framework

### Weekly Priority Filtering
1. **Alignment Check**: Does this advance my monthly goals?
2. **Energy Assessment**: Do I have the mental/physical resources?
3. **Opportunity Cost**: What am I saying no to by saying yes to this?
4. **Time Boxing**: Can this be completed within available time blocks?

### Daily Task Prioritization
- **Must Do**: Non-negotiable commitments and deadlines
- **Should Do**: Important for goals but flexible timing  
- **Could Do**: Nice to have but not essential
- **Won't Do**: Consciously choosing not to pursue

## Personal Operating Principles

### Work Philosophy
- **Systems over goals** - Build processes that create sustainable results
- **Quality over quantity** - Better to do fewer things exceptionally well
- **Learning over earning** - Prioritize growth opportunities that compound
- **Automation over optimization** - Eliminate tasks rather than just improving them

### Life Balance Approach
- **Integration not balance** - Weave together rather than separate completely
- **Seasonal focus** - Some periods require more professional intensity
- **Recovery periods** - Schedule downtime proactively, not reactively
- **Relationship investment** - Consistent small gestures over grand gestures

## Habit Stack Architecture

### Morning Stack (Linked Sequence)
1. Wake up ‚Üí Immediately drink 16oz water
2. Water ‚Üí 5 minutes stretching or movement  
3. Stretching ‚Üí Coffee preparation
4. Coffee ‚Üí Review daily priorities
5. Priorities ‚Üí Start most important task

### Evening Stack (Linked Sequence)  
1. Work end ‚Üí Close all apps and clear desk
2. Clear desk ‚Üí 5-minute walk outside
3. Walk ‚Üí Change into casual clothes
4. Clothes change ‚Üí Prepare tomorrow's priorities
5. Planning ‚Üí Relaxation/personal time

## Goal Tracking & Review

### Weekly Review Questions
- What were my biggest wins this week?
- Where did I get stuck or procrastinate?
- What patterns am I noticing in my energy/productivity?
- How well did I maintain my routines?
- What should I adjust for next week?

### Monthly Assessment
- **Goal Progress**: Am I on track with major objectives?
- **System Performance**: Which routines are working/not working?
- **Energy Patterns**: When am I most/least effective?
- **Relationship Health**: Am I investing enough in key relationships?
- **Learning Growth**: What new skills or insights have I gained?

## Seasonal Adjustments

### High-Intensity Periods (Project launches, deadlines)
- Reduce optional commitments
- Increase focus block duration
- Simplify meal planning and decision-making
- Schedule recovery time immediately after

### Maintenance Periods (Steady state)
- Add learning projects and skill development
- Increase social and relationship time
- Experiment with new routines or systems
- Build buffer for future high-intensity periods

### Recovery Periods (Post-project, vacations)
- Minimize structured commitments  
- Focus on relationships and personal interests
- Reflect on lessons learned and system improvements
- Gradually ramp back up to normal intensity
